Special Register

What is
Special Register

Special registers are specific types of registers in a computer that have unique roles in managing the operation of the processor. Unlike general-purpose registers, which hold data for calculations, special registers are used for controlling the flow of execution and managing important system functions. Examples of special registers include the Program Counter (PC), which keeps track of the address of the next instruction to execute, and the Stack Pointer (SP), which points to the top of the stack in memory. These registers help coordinate the processor's activities and ensure that instructions are processed in the correct sequence.
